Travel Tips for Kazakhstan

Essential information for planning your journey

Planning Your Trip to Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan offers a unique travel experience with its blend of ancient traditions and modern development. Here's everything you need to know to make your journey smooth and enjoyable.

Visa & Entry Requirements

Many countries enjoy visa-free travel to Kazakhstan for stays of up to 30 days, including the US, UK, EU countries, Australia, and Canada. Always check the latest visa requirements before traveling as policies can change.

  • Your passport should be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure date
  • All foreign visitors must register with migration authorities if staying more than 5 days
  • Keep a copy of your passport and visa (if applicable) separate from the originals

Best Time to Visit

Kazakhstan has a continental climate with hot summers and cold winters. The best time to visit is during late spring (May-June) and early autumn (September-October) when temperatures are mild and comfortable for sightseeing.

  • Winter (December-February): Very cold with temperatures dropping to -20¬∞C (-4¬∞F) or lower
  • Spring/Autumn: Mild temperatures and fewer tourists

Transportation

Kazakhstan is a vast country, and traveling between major cities often requires flights. Within cities, taxis, buses, and ride-sharing services are available.

  • Air Travel: Air Astana and SCAT Airlines offer domestic flights between major cities
  • Trains: Comfortable and affordable for long-distance travel, including overnight trains
  • Taxis: Readily available in cities; agree on a price before starting your journey
  • Ride-sharing: Yandex Taxi and InDriver are popular and affordable options in major cities

Accommodation

Major cities like Nur-Sultan and Almaty offer a range of accommodation options from luxury hotels to budget hostels. In rural areas, options may be limited, so book in advance.

  • Hotels: International chains and local options available in major cities
  • Hostels: Budget-friendly options in tourist areas
  • Guesthouses: Good options in smaller towns and rural areas
  • Yurt Stays: For an authentic experience, consider staying in a traditional yurt in rural areas

Money & Costs

The currency in Kazakhstan is the Tenge (KZT). Credit cards are widely accepted in cities, but cash is preferred in rural areas. ATMs are readily available in urban centers.

  • Exchange money at banks or official exchange offices for the best rates
  • Notify your bank of your travel plans to avoid card blocks
  • Tipping is not customary but becoming more common in tourist areas (10% is sufficient)
  • Kazakhstan is relatively affordable compared to Western Europe or North America

Health & Safety

Kazakhstan is generally safe for travelers, with low crime rates in tourist areas. Basic precautions should be taken, especially in crowded places. Healthcare facilities in major cities are adequate, but may be limited in rural areas.

  • Purchase comprehensive travel insurance before your trip
  • Bring any prescription medications you need (with prescriptions)
  • Tap water is not recommended for drinking; stick to bottled water
  • Emergency number: 112 (for police, fire, and ambulance)

Cultural Etiquette

Kazakhs are known for their hospitality and friendliness toward visitors. Understanding and respecting local customs will enhance your experience and help you connect with locals.

Do's

  • Accept offers of tea or food when visiting someone's home
  • Remove your shoes when entering a private home
  • Use your right hand or both hands when giving or receiving items
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Kazakh or Russian
  • Respect elders and give them precedence

Don'ts

  • Refuse hospitality or food offerings (at least taste a little)
  • Discuss politics or criticize the government in public
  • Take photographs of government buildings or military installations
  • Point the soles of your feet toward others when sitting
  • Show public displays of affection
  • Drink alcohol in public places (outside of restaurants/bars)

Essential Packing List

What you pack will depend on the season of your visit, but here are some essentials for any trip to Kazakhstan:

Documents & Essentials

  • Passport and visa (if required)
  • Travel insurance documents
  • Flight and accommodation confirmations
  • Credit/debit cards and some cash
  • Power adapter (Type C and F plugs)
  • Prescription medications

Clothing

  • Layered clothing (weather can change quickly)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Modest clothing for religious sites
  • Warm jacket (even in summer for mountain areas)
  • Hat and sunglasses
  • Swimwear (for lakes or hotel pools)

Other Items

  • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Basic first aid kit
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes
  • Camera with extra batteries
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Offline maps and translation apps
