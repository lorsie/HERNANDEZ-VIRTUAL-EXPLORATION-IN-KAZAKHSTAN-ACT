Top 5 Attractions

Must-visit destinations in Kazakhstan

Discover Kazakhstan's Natural Wonders and Cultural Landmarks

From breathtaking canyons to modern architectural marvels, Kazakhstan offers a diverse range of attractions for every traveler. Here are the top five destinations you shouldn't miss during your visit.

alt="Charyn Canyon" class="w-full h-full object-cover" />
1

Charyn Canyon

Often called Kazakhstan's Grand Canyon, Charyn Canyon stretches for 80 kilometers along the Charyn River. Its most famous section, the Valley of Castles, features stunning rock formations that resemble ancient fortresses and towers, sculpted by wind and water over millions of years.

The canyon's red sandstone cliffs reach heights of up to 300 meters, creating a dramatic landscape that's perfect for photography, hiking, and camping. The area is also home to diverse plant and animal species, some of which are rare and endemic to the region.

Visitor Information

  • Location: 200 km east of Almaty
  • Best time to visit: April to October
  • Activities: Hiking, photography, camping
  • Tip: Bring plenty of water and sun protection as there is limited shade in the canyon
2

Kolsai Lakes

Known as the "Pearls of Tien Shan," the Kolsai Lakes are a system of three alpine lakes nestled in the northern Tien Shan Mountains. Surrounded by dense coniferous forests and snow-capped peaks, these crystal-clear lakes offer some of Kazakhstan's most picturesque scenery.

Each lake sits at a different altitude, connected by hiking trails that allow visitors to explore the pristine mountain environment. The area is perfect for nature lovers, offering opportunities for hiking, horseback riding, fishing, and camping in a serene natural setting.

Visitor Information

  • Location: 300 km southeast of Almaty
  • Best time to visit: May to September
  • Activities: Hiking, horseback riding, fishing, camping
  • Tip: Consider hiring a local guide to fully explore the area and learn about its ecology

3

Bayterek Tower

Standing at 97 meters tall, the Bayterek Tower is the iconic symbol of Nur-Sultan (formerly Astana), Kazakhstan's modern capital city. This futuristic monument represents a Kazakh legend about a mythical bird of happiness that laid its egg in the branches of a poplar tree.

Visitors can take an elevator to the observation deck inside the "egg" at the top of the tower, offering panoramic views of the city's striking architecture. The tower is particularly impressive at night when it's illuminated with colorful lights, making it a must-visit landmark in Kazakhstan's capital.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Central Nur-Sultan
  • Best time to visit: Year-round (evening visits recommended for light displays)
  • Activities: Observation deck, photography, nearby restaurants and shops
  • Tip: Place your hand in the golden handprint of Kazakhstan's first president at the top for good luck
4

Medeu and Shymbulak

Located just outside Almaty, Medeu is the world's highest Olympic-sized ice skating rink, sitting at an elevation of 1,691 meters above sea level. Built in 1972, the rink has been the venue for numerous speed skating world records and continues to attract both professional athletes and recreational skaters.

Just a short cable car ride above Medeu is Shymbulak, Kazakhstan's premier ski resort. With slopes reaching up to 3,200 meters and offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains, Shymbulak provides excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities from November to April, while in summer, it transforms into a hiking and mountain biking destination.

Visitor Information

  • Location: 15 km south of Almaty
  • Best time to visit: Winter for skiing and skating; summer for hiking
  • Activities: Ice skating, skiing, snowboarding, hiking, cable car rides
  • Tip: Take the 4.5 km cable car ride from Medeu to Shymbulak for spectacular views

5

Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi

Located in the city of Turkistan, this UNESCO World Heritage site is one of Kazakhstan's most significant historical and architectural monuments. Built in the late 14th century by order of Timur (Tamerlane), the mausoleum honors Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, a Sufi master who significantly influenced the development of Islamic culture in Central Asia.

The mausoleum's impressive scale and intricate tilework make it a masterpiece of medieval architecture. Despite remaining unfinished after Timur's death, it stands as one of the best-preserved examples of Timurid architecture and continues to be an important pilgrimage site for Muslims from across Central Asia.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Turkistan, southern Kazakhstan
  • Best time to visit: April to October
  • Activities: Guided tours, cultural and historical exploration
  • Tip: Dress modestly as this is a religious site; women should cover their heads when entering
